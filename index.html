About Me

I am a first-year Master’s student in AIGS at UNIST, advised by Professor Jeong-hwan Jeon.

I received my Bachelors degree in Artificial Intelligence at Catholic University of Korea. Previously, I spent some time at Applied AI Lab as an undergraduate research intern, where I developed reinforcement learning algorithms for ride-sharing using Seoul city bus stop datasets.

Research Vision

My research focuses on developing AI and robotics systems capable of complex manipulation and long-horizon planning tasks. I am interested in reinforcement learning, robot learning, and real-world applications of intelligent robotics.

  • Which low-level motor control skills should be pretrained for robots?
  • How can these skills be composed to perform long-horizon tasks?
  • How to effectively transfer simulation-learned policies to real-world robots?
